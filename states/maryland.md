## Detailed entries

### Baltimore County

- **Type:** County
- **Status:** 🟢 Active
- **Date enacted:** 2026-02-02 council enactment; applies retroactively to 2026-01-05 introduction
- **Duration:** Until the earlier of 2027-01-01 or 90 days after the Planning Board submits its 
- **Moratorium ID:** `md-baltimore-county-2026`
- **Legal basis:** Bill 3-26, Zoning Regulations - Data Center Study
- **What prompted it:** County concern that Bill 54-24 had opened broad zoning pathways before the county had studied electricity demand, public water demand, siting, notice, public health, environmental, and incentive issues
- **Affected projects:** Countywide suspension on accepting or approving development plans, zoning petitions, permits, and other approvals for data centers
- **Detailed status:** Active
- **Outcome:** Planning Board report due by 2026-10-01; possible recommendations include a ban, siting controls, permitting changes, notice requirements, and incentive limits

### Prince George's County

- **Type:** County
- **Status:** ⚪ Expired
- **Date enacted:** 2025-09-16 council resolution; 2026-01-23 executive order extension
- **Duration:** CR-098-2025: 180 days or until comprehensive legislation; EO 1-2026: temporary h
- **Moratorium ID:** `md-prince-george-s-county-2025`
- **Legal basis:** CR-098-2025; Executive Order 1-2026; underlying task force created by CR-016-2025
- **What prompted it:** Community concerns and task force review of energy demand, ratepayer impacts, air, water, woodland, quality-of-life, revenue, and siting issues
- **Affected projects:** Preliminary plans of subdivision that include qualified data centers; executive hold on accepting, considering, and processing qualified data center permit applications
- **Detailed status:** CR pause (180 days from 2025-09-16) has expired by operation of its own term; EO 1-2026 permit hold remains active through 2026-04-30
- **Outcome:** Task force final report issued in November 2025; status of comprehensive replacement legislation
